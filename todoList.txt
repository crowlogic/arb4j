The Newton flow for f is given by:(d*z)/(d*t)=-(f(z(t)))/(f'(z(t)))∀ z∈ℂ,t∈ℝ  (1)Wick rotation: t↦-i*τ, with τ∈ℝ (imaginary time).The Schrödinger equation in imaginary time:-(∂*ψ)/(∂*τ)=H*=-1/(2*m)*∇^2+V(x)  (2)where H is the Hamiltonian operatorConditions for Equivalence To establish an equivalence between the Newton flow under Wick rotation and solutions of the Schrödinger equation, we require:1.Analytic continuation of the Newton flow: f(z) and f'(z) admit extensions to functions of τ, ensuring the flow equation remains well-defined under t↦-i*τ.2.Quadratic Hamiltonian correspondence: Existence of a potential function V(z) derived from f(z), such that the HamiltonianH=-1/(2*m)*∇^2+V(z)  (3) can generate dynamics equivalent to those described by the Newton flow after Wick rotation.3.Initial and boundary condition mapping: Initial and boundary conditions for the Newton flow must map to physically meaningful conditions for the Schrödinger equation, ensuring solutions are comparable.Formal Expression Given a holomorphic function f(z), if there exists a potential function V(z) such that the HamiltonianH=-1/(2*m)*∇^2+V(z)  (4)yields a Schrödinger equation in imaginary time,-(∂*ψ)/(∂*τ)=(-1/(2*m)*∇^2+V(z))*ψ(z,τ)  (5)whose solutions ψ(z,τ) exhibit dynamics that can be directly related to the trajectories z(τ) defined by the analytically continued Newton flow(d*z)/(d*τ)=-i*(f(z))/(f'(z))  (6)then the Newton flow is said to be equivalent to solutions of the Schrödinger equation under the specified potential V(z).Solution for ψ(z,τ) The symbolic solution to the Schrödinger equation for ψ(z,τ), expressed in terms of differential operators and the potential V(z), is given as:ψ(z,τ)=(-m⋅(∂*ψ)/(∂*τ)+1/2⋅(∂^2*ψ)/(∂*z^2))/(m⋅V(z))  (7)This expression illustrates the relationship between the wave function ψ, its derivatives with respect to time (τ) and space (z), and the potential V(z).
