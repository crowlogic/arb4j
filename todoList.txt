Applying Rice's formula **correctly** to the stationary process $$X$$:$$\mathbb{E}[N_{Z,[a,b]}] = \mathbb{E}[N_{X,[\theta(a),\theta(b)]}] = \frac{\theta(b) - \theta(a)}{\pi} \sqrt{\frac{-\ddot{K}(0)}{K(0)}}$$3. The abstract formula $$\mathbb{E}[N_{[a,b]}] = \sqrt{-\ddot{K}(0)} \cdot (\theta(b) - \theta(a))$$ is **missing**:   - The normalization factor $$1/\pi$$   - The variance term $$K(0)$$ in the denominator## Verdict❌ **The zero-counting formula as stated in the abstract is INCORRECT** (or at minimum, incomplete)**Correct formula**:$$\mathbb{E}[N_{[a,b]}] = \frac{\theta(b) - \theta(a)}{\pi} \sqrt{\frac{-\ddot{K}(0)}{K(0)}}$$The variance $$K(0)$$ cannot be omitted unless explicitly assuming unit variance, which is not stated anywhere in the document.
