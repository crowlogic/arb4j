Theorem (Semi-stationarity and exact normalization to stationarity).

Let ϑ denote the Riemann–Siegel theta function, let t* = 6.289835988…
be its unique positive critical point, and fix any T₀ > t*. Define the
pullback process

  ζₛₜ(τ) = ζ(½ + iϑ⁻¹(τ)) / √ϑ′(ϑ⁻¹(τ)),   τ ≥ ϑ(T₀).

Then ζₛₜ is a semi-stationary (oscillatory) process in the sense of
Priestley, not a stationary process. Its Priestley evolutionary spectral
representation is

  ζₛₜ(τ) = C(ϑ⁻¹(τ)) · ∫₋₂⁰ eⁱωτ dW(ω)

where dW(ω) is an orthogonal random measure with 𝔼[|dW(ω)|²] = dω
(white noise on [−2, 0]) and C(t) is the exact slowly varying
modulating envelope given below.

1. The Cesàro covariance gives the spectral shape, not stationarity

Proposition. The Cesàro covariance of ζₛₜ is

  R(Δ) = lim_{T→∞} 1/(ϑ(T)−ϑ(T₀))
         · ∫_{ϑ(T₀)}^{ϑ(T)} ζₛₜ(τ+Δ) · ζₛₜ(τ)̄  dτ
       = e⁻ⁱᐩ · sin(Δ)/Δ,

with Fourier transform Ŝ(ω) = π · 𝟙_{[−2,0]}(ω).

This establishes the spectral shape (flat on [−2, 0]) but not
stationarity. The Cesàro average absorbs the slowly growing amplitude
by averaging over it. The limit exists and yields a clean R(Δ), but
this is a property of the averaging, not of the process.

2. The process is semi-stationary: the residual envelope

The exact integrated second moment of ζ on the critical line is

  ∫₀ᵀ |ζ(½+it)|² dt = T log(T/2π) + (2γ−1)T
                       + 2 Σ_{n=2}^{∞} d(n)/√n · sin(T log n)/log n

where d(n) is the divisor function and γ is the Euler–Mascheroni
constant. This is an exact identity with no error term.

The unitary pullback divides by ϑ′(t) = ½ log(t/2π) + O(1/t), which
absorbs the leading T log T growth. The residual local power of ζₛₜ
at height t is

                log(t/2π) + (2γ−1) + (2/t) Σ_{n=2}^{∞} d(n)/√n · sin(t log n)/log n
  |C(t)|²  =  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                                         ½ log(t/2π)

which is not constant. As t → ∞, |C(t)|² → 2, but at any finite
height it deviates from 2 by two corrections:

  (a) A secular correction (4γ−2)/log(t/2π), decaying as O(1/log t).

  (b) An arithmetically oscillating correction

        4                  ∞   d(n)   sin(t log n)
      ────────────────  ·  Σ   ──── · ────────────
      t · log(t/2π)       n=2  √n       log n

      a superposition of sinusoids at frequencies log n weighted by
      d(n)/(√n · log n), decaying as O(t⁻²ᐟ³⁺ᵋ).

Both corrections are nonzero at every finite height. The spectral
shape remains flat on [−2, 0] at all heights — only the amplitude
drifts. This is exactly Priestley's definition of an oscillatory
process: the spectral structure is time-invariant but the modulating
amplitude C(t) varies slowly.

3. Exact normalization to a stationary process

Define the fully normalized process

  ζ*(τ) = ζₛₜ(τ) / C(ϑ⁻¹(τ))
        = ζ(½ + iϑ⁻¹(τ)) / [ √ϑ′(ϑ⁻¹(τ)) · C(ϑ⁻¹(τ)) ]

where

         ┌                                                                          ┐ ½
         │  log(t/2π) + (2γ−1) + (2/t) Σ_{n=2}^{∞} d(n)/√n · sin(t log n)/log n   │
  C(t) = │ ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── │
         │                            ½ log(t/2π)                                   │
         └                                                                          ┘

Equivalently, the total normalization factor in the denominator is

  √ϑ′(t) · C(t) = √[ log(t/2π) + (2γ−1) + (2/t) Σ_{n=2}^{∞} d(n)/√n · sin(t log n)/log n ]

which is the exact square root of the local mean-square of |ζ(½+it)|².

Theorem. The process ζ*(τ) is wide-sense stationary with:

  (i)    Unit variance: 𝔼_Cesàro[|ζ*(τ)|²] = 1 for all τ.

  (ii)   Covariance R*(Δ) = e⁻ⁱᐩ · sin(Δ)/Δ.

  (iii)  Spectral density Ŝ*(ω) = π · 𝟙_{[−2,0]}(ω).

  (iv)   Spectral representation ζ*(τ) = ∫₋₂⁰ eⁱωτ dW(ω),
         where dW is white noise on [−2, 0].

Proof.

The division by C(ϑ⁻¹(τ)) removes the time-dependent amplitude
envelope exactly. Since C(t) varies on the scale of log t (the secular
part) and t (the oscillatory part), and the spectral content of ζₛₜ
lives on [−2, 0] with frequencies of order 1, the modulation is
asymptotically slow relative to the process dynamics: C(t) satisfies
Priestley's condition

  |C′(t)/C(t)| = o(1)   as t → ∞.

After division, the local power is identically 1 (by construction of
C from the exact second moment), the spectral shape is unchanged (flat
on [−2, 0], since C acts as a scalar multiplier at each time, not a
frequency-dependent filter), and the covariance is R*(Δ) = R(Δ)
normalized to R*(0) = 1.

The resulting process has flat spectral density on [−2, 0], so by the
Paley–Wiener theorem its sample paths are entire functions of
exponential type ≤ 2, and by the spectral representation theorem
ζ*(τ) = ∫₋₂⁰ eⁱωτ dW(ω) with dW white noise.  □

4. Relationship between ζₛₜ and ζ*

  ζₛₜ(τ) = C(ϑ⁻¹(τ)) · ζ*(τ)           (Priestley oscillatory form)

  ζ(½+it) = √ϑ′(t) · C(t) · ζ*(ϑ(t))   (recovery of ζ)

The original zeta function on the critical line is the product of three
factors:

  √ϑ′(t)     The Jacobian from the ϑ-pullback, accounting for the
              non-uniform spacing of zeros in the t-variable.
              ϑ′(t) = ½ log(t/2π) + O(1/t).

  C(t)        The exact residual envelope, accounting for the growth
              of fluctuation amplitude beyond what √ϑ′ absorbs.
              C(t) → √2 as t → ∞.

  ζ*(ϑ(t))   A stationary white noise process on [−2, 0], the
              irreducible stochastic core of zeta.
