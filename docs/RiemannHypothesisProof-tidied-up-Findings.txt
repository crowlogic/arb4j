# Mathematical Review: A Proof of the Riemann Hypothesis via Band-Limited Spectral Analysis of the Hardy Z-Function (tidied-up)

## Findings

### Finding 1 — Fixable
- **Location**: Proof of Theorem thm:cov, part (I) "Off-diagonal difference-frequency".
- **Claim as written**: After applying Montgomery–Vaughan to ∑_{n≤N} a_n n^{it} with a_n = n^{−1/2}, the off-diagonal cross terms in X(u+h)X(u) contribute O(T^{−1/2}) → 0 after normalization by U ≍ T log T.
- **What is relied on but not established in the text**: Reduction of ∫₀^T X(u+h)X(u) du off-diagonal to a quadratic form ∫₀^T |∑_n a_n n^{it}|² dt on which the stated Montgomery–Vaughan inequality applies. The off-diagonal phases φ_m(u+h) − φ_n(u) = [θ(t(u+h)) − θ(t(u))] − t(u+h) log m + t(u) log n do not collapse to (mn)^{it} form because t(u+h) ≠ t(u) and the θ-difference does not cancel.
- **What would establish it**: A separate lemma reducing the off-diagonal integral to a form on which MV applies — for instance, by Taylor-expanding θ(t(u+h)) − θ(t(u)) and t(u+h) in h to extract a multiplicative factor (mn)^{it(u)} · e^{iψ(u,h,m,n)} with ψ uniformly controlled, followed by Cauchy–Schwarz against the diagonal MV mean square.

### Finding 2 — Fixable
- **Location**: Proof of Lemma lem:Xeps(ii), the convolution identity (eq:convid).
- **Claim as written**: X̂_ε^{(a)} = X̂_W ∗ η̂_ε^{(a)} pointwise.
- **What is relied on but not established in the text**: Compatibility of normalization between (a) the Wiener spectral density X̂_W = ½·𝟏_{[−1,1]} defined by K(h) = ∫ e^{ihξ} μ(dξ) and (b) the distributional Fourier transform of X under the convention 𝓕{f}(ξ) = ∫ f(h) e^{−ihξ} dh. The convolution theorem for the product of a tempered distribution with a Schwartz function reads f̂g = (2π)^{−1} f̂ ∗ ĝ under this convention; the displayed identity has no (2π)^{−1} factor.
- **What would establish it**: An explicit statement that X̂_W in (eq:convid) denotes the appropriate normalization of the spectral density (e.g., 2π times the density of the Conventions section) so that the constants match, with the convolution theorem for η_ε^{(a)} ∈ 𝓢 ⊂ 𝓢' times X ∈ 𝓢' named with its prefactor.

### Finding 3 — Fixable
- **Location**: Proof of Lemma lem:Xeps(iii).
- **Claim as written**: lim_{ε→0⁺} M_b^{(a)}(ε) = (e^b − 1)/b by dominated convergence "dominated by a fixed Schwartz majorant".
- **What is relied on but not established in the text**: A function g ∈ L¹(ℝ) independent of ε ∈ (0, ε₀] with e^{b|ξ|} X̂_ε^{(a)}(ξ) ≤ g(ξ) for all such ε. The O_a(·) decay constants in Lemma lem:eta(iii) and Lemma lem:Xeps(ii) are not stated as uniform in ε, and the explicit Fourier transform of e^{−ε√(a²+u²)} has ε-dependent prefactors.
- **What would establish it**: An ε-uniform pointwise bound X̂_ε^{(a)}(ξ) ≤ C_{a,ε₀} |ξ|^{−1/2} e^{−a|ξ|} (or any integrable bound exceeding e^{−b|ξ|} in the tails for b < a) for ε ∈ (0, ε₀], derived from the explicit formula for η̂_ε^{(a)} and its convolution with ½·𝟏_{[−1,1]}.

### Finding 4 — Fixable
- **Location**: Proof of Theorem thm:poseps, identification of F_{n,ε}.
- **Claim as written**: F_{n,ε} is the autocorrelation of d ↦ d^n X̂_ε(d)^{1/2} in the convolution sense, hence F̂_{n,ε} = |(d^n X̂_ε^{1/2})̂|² ≥ 0.
- **What is relied on but not established in the text**: That F_{n,ε}(s) = ∫ d^{2n} X̂_ε((s+d)/2) X̂_ε((s−d)/2) dd equals ∫ g(τ) g(τ−s) dτ for g(τ) = τ^n X̂_ε(τ)^{1/2}. Substituting τ = (s+d)/2 into the autocorrelation produces an integrand with factor (d² − s²)^n · X̂_ε((s+d)/2)^{1/2} X̂_ε((s−d)/2)^{1/2}, not d^{2n} · X̂_ε((s+d)/2) X̂_ε((s−d)/2).
- **What would establish it**: A direct computation of F̂_{n,ε}(u) as a manifestly nonnegative quantity — for example, returning to L_n[X_ε](u) = 1/(2(2π)²) ∬ (ξ−η)^{2n} e^{iu(ξ+η)} X̂_ε(ξ) X̂_ε(η) dξ dη and exhibiting it as ∫ |h_u(ξ)|² dν(ξ) or a sum of squares via an explicit factorization, or any other named result for nonnegativity of L_n[f] when f̂ ≥ 0.

### Finding 5 — Fixable
- **Location**: Proof of Theorem thm:sumfreq, "Stationary phase in q" step.
- **Claim as written**: ∑_{q∼Q} d(q) e^{iΨ_q(t*(q))} = O(√Q log Q) by "the Voronoi-type estimate, Ivić, Ch. 3, summation formula for d(n) against oscillatory weights".
- **What is relied on but not established in the text**: The specific form of e^{iΨ_q(t*(q))} as a function of q ∼ Q — its leading phase in q, its derivative behavior, and the precise Voronoi-type bound being cited. With t*(q) = 2πq + O(q^{−1}) and θ(t) = (t/2) log(t/(2πe)) + O(t^{−1}), the leading-order behavior of Ψ_q(t*(q)) = 2θ(t*(q)) − t*(q) log q as a function of q ∈ [Q, 2Q] requires explicit expansion before invoking square-root cancellation.
- **What would establish it**: An explicit asymptotic expansion of Ψ_q(t*(q)) identifying the phase modulo 2πℤ as a function of q with controlled second derivative on q ∼ Q, followed by a precise citation to the Voronoi summation formula form (with the dual sum involving K₀, Y₀ Bessel kernels) whose hypotheses are met.
