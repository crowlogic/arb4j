1. Step 4 of thm:Acont — rewrite without "viewed as" and without fixed-point language.

Replace the current Step 4 with the following. The formal series A(z, u) is unique by Theorem thm:Muntz and satisfies

    A(z, u) = Ψ_μ(z) ⊙ B[A](z, u)

by Lemma lem:hadamard. For any compact K ⊇ K_Ψ on which A(·, u) admits meromorphic continuation to X̃ ∖ K, Lemma lem:mb produces a meromorphic continuation of Ψ_μ ⊙ B[A] to X̃ ∖ K, and the two meromorphic functions agree on the disk of convergence, hence on X̃ ∖ K by the identity principle. Lemma lem:branch_transfer shows the branch locus of A(·, u) lies in the branch locus of F(·, u) together with K_Ψ, so A(·, u) is meromorphic on X̃ ∖ K_F(u). Existence of the minimal compact K_A(u) ⊆ K_F(u) follows from Theorem thm:stahl_extremal.


2. lem:no_real_poles — derive the residue formula.

At a simple pole z*(u) of A(·, u) inherited from a discriminant zero of F(·, u), i.e.,

    (1 − z*(u) Q(u))² = 4 z*(u) P(u) R(u) Ψ_μ(z*(u)),

expand

    F(z, u) = ((1 − z Q(u)) − √((1 − z Q(u))² − 4 z P(u) R(u) Ψ_μ(z))) / (2 z R(u))

near z*(u) using

    Δ_F(z, u) = Δ_F′(z*(u), u) · (z − z*(u)) + O((z − z*(u))²),

giving

    Res(F(·, u), z*(u)) = √(−Δ_F′(z*(u), u)) / (2 z*(u) R(u)),

then transfer to A(·, u) via the branch-transfer lemma. Substituting Δ_F′(z*(u), u) using the defining relation reduces the residue to the form stated in the lemma. Write the calculation out in three lines so the lemma is self-contained.
