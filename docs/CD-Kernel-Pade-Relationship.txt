THEOREM (EXACT). Let μ be the moment functional with moments μ_k = a_{k+1} generated by the fractional Riccati recurrence D^μ y = P + Q y + R y². Let {Q_k}_{k≥0} be the monic orthogonal polynomials for μ with squared norms h_k = ‖Q_k‖²_μ, satisfying the Favard recurrence:

Q_{k+1}(z) = (z − α_k) Q_k(z) − β_k Q_{k−1}(z),  Q_0 = 1,  Q_{−1} = 0

with recurrence coefficients α_k, β_k produced by the σ-table. Let {Pn_k} be the associated polynomials of the second kind (Pn_0 = 0, Pn_1 = h_0) satisfying the same recurrence. Define reciprocal polynomials Q_k^*(z) = z^k Q_k(1/z), Pn_k^*(z) = z^{k−1} Pn_k(1/z). Then:

1. The Padé approximant is Φ(M)(z) = Pn_M^*(z) / Q_M^*(z).
2. The Christoffel–Darboux kernel is K_M(z,w) = Σ_{k=0}^{M} Q_k(z) Q_k(w) / h_k.
3. By the Christoffel–Darboux formula: K_M(z,w) = [Q_{M+1}(z) Q_M(w) − Q_M(z) Q_{M+1}(w)] / [h_{M+1} (z − w)].

GAUSSIAN QUADRATURE FOR μ. The M nodes {ζ_j}_{j=1}^M are the eigenvalues of the M×M Jacobi matrix
J_M = diag(α_0, …, α_{M−1}) + offdiag(√β_1, …, √β_{M−1}).
Equivalently, {ζ_j} are the zeros of Q_M(z). The quadrature weights are
w_j = h_0 / [Q_M'(ζ_j) Q_{M−1}(ζ_j)] = h_{M−1} / [Q_M'(ζ_j) Q_{M−1}(ζ_j)]
which satisfies Σ_{j=1}^M w_j f(ζ_j) = ∫ f dμ for all f ∈ Π_{2M−1}.

EXACT RELATIONSHIP. The Padé approximant's poles {z_j} are the reciprocals z_j = 1/ζ_j, i.e., the zeros of Q_M^*(z) = Φden(M)(z). The CD kernel evaluated at these nodes is diagonal: K_M(z_i, z_j) = δ_{ij} / w_j. The Padé approximant is the unique rational function that interpolates the Cauchy transform C_μ(z) = ∫ dμ(w)/(z−w) at the nodes {ζ_j}, equivalently interpolates C_μ(1/z) z at {z_j}. For any z not a pole,

Φ(M)(z) = Σ_{j=1}^M [Pn_M^*(z_j) / Q_M^*'(z_j)] · K_M(z, z_j) · w_j.

PROOF. The zeros of Q_M are the eigenvalues of J_M (Gautschi 2004, Thm 1.32). The weights w_j = h_0 / (Q_M'(ζ_j) Q_{M−1}(ζ_j)) are standard (Gautschi 2004, Eq. 1.4.14). The Padé approximant Pn_M/Q_M interpolates C_μ at {ζ_j} (Brezinski 1980, Thm 3.1.1). Reciprocal polynomials map this to Φ(M)(z) = Pn_M^*(z)/Q_M^*(z) interpolating C_μ(1/z)z at {z_j}. The CD kernel K_M(z,w) is the reproducing kernel for Π_M in L²(μ), so K_M(z_j, ·) are the Lagrange basis polynomials for {z_j} with K_M(z_i, z_j) = δ_{ij}/w_j. Substituting the interpolation formula Φ(M)(z) = Σ_j Φ(M)(z_j) K_M(z, z_j)/K_M(z_j, z_j) and using Φ(M)(z_j) = Pn_M^*(z_j)/Q_M^*'(z_j) (residue at simple pole) gives the stated formula. ∎
