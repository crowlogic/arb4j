  Step 4 (Branch locus). The formal series A(z, u) is unique by Theorem
  thm:Muntz and satisfies A(z, u) = Ψ_μ(z) ⊙ B[A](z, u) by Lemma lem:hadamard.

  compact K_A(u) ⊆ K_F(u) follows from Theorem thm:stahl_extremal.

2. lem:no_real_poles — derive the residue formula

At a simple pole z*(u) of A(·, u) inherited from a discriminant zero of
F(·, u), i.e.,

    (1 − z*(u) Q(u))² = 4 z*(u) P(u) R(u) Ψ_μ(z*(u)),

expand

                  (1 − z Q(u)) − √( (1 − z Q(u))² − 4 z P(u) R(u) Ψ_μ(z) )
    F(z, u)  =  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                                       2 z R(u)

near z*(u) using

    Δ_F(z, u) = Δ_F'(z*(u), u) · (z − z*(u)) + O((z − z*(u))²),

giving

                                    √( −Δ_F'(z*(u), u) )
    Res( F(·, u), z*(u) )  =  ──────────────────────────── ,
                                      2 z*(u) R(u)

then transfer to A(·, u) via the branch-transfer lemma; substituting
Δ_F'(z*(u), u) using the defining relation reduces the residue to the form
stated in the lemma. Write the calculation out in three lines so the lemma
is self-contained.

K_A, K_F are inconsistently written with and without the u argument. Unify:

  · every occurrence of P, Q, R is written P(u), Q(u), R(u);
  · every occurrence of K_A, K_F is written K_A(u), K_F(u);
  · every occurrence of Δ_F is written Δ_F(z, u);
  · every occurrence of F, A is written F(z, u), A(z, u);

    u argument.

functions and the entire pipeline depends on u symbolically.
