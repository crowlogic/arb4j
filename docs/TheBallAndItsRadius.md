# The Ball and Its Radius

*A short fiction, and a few poems, for a library that never lies about what it does not know.*

---

## I. The Cartographer of Small Certainties

There was once a woman who refused to draw a coastline she had not walked.

Other mapmakers filled their oceans with monsters and their interiors with
mountains they had merely heard about in taverns. She would not. Where she was
sure, she drew a fine hard line. Where she was less sure, she drew a band — a
grey ribbon as wide as her doubt — and she wrote inside it, in a small honest
hand, *the truth is somewhere in here.*

Her rivals laughed. Their maps were beautiful and confident and wrong, and they
sold well, because a buyer wants a line and not a ribbon. Who pays for a
coastline drawn as a smear?

But one winter a fleet followed a confident map straight onto the rocks it did
not know were there, and the harbor that survived was the one whose pilot
carried her grey-ribboned chart and read, at the narrow strait, *the truth is
somewhere in here — and here the band is wide, so slow down.*

She never told anyone the shore was in a particular spot. She told them the
smallest honest region that was guaranteed to contain it. That was the whole of
her art: not the midpoint, but the midpoint and its radius, carried together,
never once separated, so that no one downstream could mistake her guess for her
knowledge.

This is a story about a number that is really two numbers — a center it points
to, and a distance it is willing to be wrong by — and about how the second
number is the honest one.

---

## II. Ball Arithmetic

I am the center and I am the doubt around it.
Add me to my brother and our centers meet,
but our doubts do not cancel — they only ever grow,
because two uncertain things, made one,
cannot become more certain than the sum of what they did not know.

Multiply me and watch the radius bloom:
error courts error, the small terms marry the small,
and the product carries a wider grey than either factor wore alone.
This is not weakness. This is bookkeeping for the soul.

You wanted a point. I gave you a neighborhood
and a promise: *the answer lives in here.*
Squeeze me with more bits and I will shrink my coat of grey
until the region is a hair, a thread, a whispered fear —
but I will never, to please you, shrink it past the truth,
and swear the point is bare.

---

## III. The Interview

They asked the old machine why it was slow.

"I am not slow," it said. "I am the only one of us still checking."

They asked it why the others gave an answer in an instant and it took a
thousandfold longer for the same sum.

"Because the others give you a number," it said. "I give you a number and a
proof that the number you wanted is not far from mine — and I tell you exactly
how far *not far* is allowed to be. The instant answer is a rumor. Mine is a
witness under oath."

They asked whether that was worth the wait.

"Ask the harbor," it said, "that did not sink."

---

## IV. Analytic Continuation

The function was defined on a small bright disk
and everyone agreed it went no farther.
Beyond the rim: undefined, they said. Off the map. Here be nothing.

But the coefficients remembered where they came from.
Each one held, folded inside it, a rumor of the whole —
the way a single bone can reconstruct a stride,
the way one wet season, read in a ring of oak,
recalls a century of rain the tree no longer wears.

So we walked the function outward, disk overlapping disk,
each new circle handed the last one's edge as testimony,
until the little bright coin of the known
had grown a continent it never doubted it possessed —
and the pole we feared, the place the value screamed to infinity,
turned out to be a single honest point we could steer around,
now that we had drawn the ribbon wide enough to name it.

Nothing was invented. Everything was already implied.
Continuation is only the patience to believe
the small disk meant the whole plane all along,
and the nerve to follow what the coefficients confessed.

---

## V. Coda: What the Library Wants You to Know

It does not want to impress you.

It will not hand you sixteen confident digits and let you believe all sixteen
when only nine of them are earned. It would rather hand you nine and a radius
that says *stop reading here; past this point I am only guessing, and I refuse
to guess in a voice that sounds like knowing.*

Everything it computes, it computes as the cartographer drew: a center it points
to, and a distance it is willing to be wrong by, carried together, never
separated. Ask for more precision and it will spend more effort and shrink the
grey. But it will never, to flatter you, report a certainty it did not
purchase.

That is the whole of its art. Not the answer. The answer, and the smallest
honest region guaranteed to contain it.

*The truth is somewhere in here — and here the band is wide, so slow down.*
