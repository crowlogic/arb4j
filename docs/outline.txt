§4  Covariance and spectral density
─────────────────────────────────────

    Proposition (Covariance):

        R(Δ) = e^{−iΔ} sin(Δ)/Δ.

    Proof: the 7-step computation.

        Step 1:  Substitution τ = ϑ(t), produce the integrand
                 ζ(½+i(t+δ)) ζ(½+it)̄  · ϑ′(t) / [√μ(t+δ) √μ(t)].

        Step 2:  Insert AFE.  Four terms I, II, III, IV.

        Step 3:  Term I diagonal.  Spectral variable
                 ω = −log n / ϑ′(t) ∈ [−1, 0].  Contribution:
                 ∫₋₁⁰ e^{iωΔ} dω.

        Step 4:  Term IV diagonal.  Phase shift by e^{−2iϑ(t)}
                 maps spectral variable to ω ∈ [−2, −1].
                 Contribution: ∫₋₂^{−1} e^{iωΔ} dω.

        Step 5:  Combined diagonal: ∫₋₂⁰ e^{iωΔ} dω = 2e^{−iΔ} sinΔ.
                 The local power factor from Terms I+IV is μ(t)
                 (this is the content of Ingham's identity).
                 After dividing by √μ(t+δ)√μ(t), the local factor
                 is 1.  Cesàro average over ϑ′(t) dt / ϑ(T) gives
                 R(Δ) = e^{−iΔ} sinΔ/Δ.

        Step 6:  Off-diagonal and cross terms vanish
                 (Montgomery–Vaughan, Riemann–Lebesgue).

        Step 7:  Fourier inversion → Ŝ(ω) = π · 𝟙_{[-2,0]}(ω).
