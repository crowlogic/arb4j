Theorem (Unitarily time-changed stationary processes are oscillatory).  
Let X be a zero-mean stationary process on ℝ with covariance

  R_X(τ) = ∫_{ℝ} e^{iλτ} f_X(λ) dλ,

and let θ: ℝ → ℝ be C¹ with θ′(t) > 0. Define

  Y(t) = √θ′(t) · X(θ(t)).

Let C_Y(t,s) denote the covariance of Y. As proved in the covariance
theorem, we have

  C_Y(t,s) = √(θ′(t)θ′(s)) · R_X(θ(t) − θ(s)).

Then Y is an oscillatory process in the sense of Priestley, with
oscillatory kernel

  A_t(λ) = √θ′(t) · e^{iλ(θ(t) − t)},

and evolutionary spectrum

  dF_Y(t,λ) = |A_t(λ)|² f_X(λ) dλ
            = θ′(t) f_X(λ) dλ.

Proof.

From the covariance theorem for the unitarily time-changed stationary
process, the covariance of Y is

  C_Y(t,s) = √(θ′(t)θ′(s)) · R_X(θ(t) − θ(s)).

Insert the spectral representation of R_X:

  R_X(θ(t) − θ(s)) = ∫_{ℝ} e^{iλ(θ(t) − θ(s))} f_X(λ) dλ.

Therefore

  C_Y(t,s) = √(θ′(t)θ′(s)) ∫_{ℝ} e^{iλ(θ(t) − θ(s))} f_X(λ) dλ.

Bring the prefactor inside the integral:

  C_Y(t,s) = ∫_{ℝ} √θ′(t) e^{iλθ(t)} · √θ′(s) e^{-iλθ(s)} f_X(λ) dλ.

Write

  √θ′(t) e^{iλθ(t)} = A_t(λ) e^{iλt},

where

  A_t(λ) = √θ′(t) · e^{iλ(θ(t) − t)}.

Then

  √θ′(s) e^{-iλθ(s)} = A_s(λ)̄ e^{-iλs},

and hence

  C_Y(t,s) = ∫_{ℝ} A_t(λ) e^{iλt} · A_s(λ)̄ e^{-iλs} f_X(λ) dλ
           = ∫_{ℝ} A_t(λ) A_s(λ)̄ e^{iλ(t−s)} f_X(λ) dλ.

This is exactly Priestley’s oscillatory covariance form: there exists a
family {A_t(λ)} such that

  C_Y(t,s) = ∫_{ℝ} A_t(λ) A_s(λ)̄ e^{iλ(t−s)} dF(λ),

with dF(λ) = f_X(λ) dλ. Consequently Y is an oscillatory process in
Priestley’s sense.

For the evolutionary spectrum, Priestley defines, for an oscillatory
process with kernel A_t(λ) and base measure dF(λ), the evolutionary
spectrum at time t by

  dF_Y(t,λ) = |A_t(λ)|² dF(λ).

Here

  dF(λ) = f_X(λ) dλ,

and

  A_t(λ) = √θ′(t) · e^{iλ(θ(t) − t)}.

Thus

  |A_t(λ)|² = |√θ′(t)|² · |e^{iλ(θ(t) − t)}|²
             = θ′(t),

so

  dF_Y(t,λ) = θ′(t) f_X(λ) dλ,

and the evolutionary spectral density is

  f_Y(t,λ) = θ′(t) f_X(λ).

The covariance C_Y(t,s) of the unitarily time-changed stationary
process therefore has the oscillatory form required by Priestley, with
oscillatory kernel A_t(λ) determined by θ, and the corresponding
evolutionary spectrum is θ′(t) f_X(λ) dλ.  ∎
