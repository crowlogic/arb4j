\section*{Setup}

Let $\theta(t)$ be the Riemann--Siegel theta function and $\Theta(t)=\theta(t)+Ct$
with $C>\tfrac{\log\pi}{2}$ so that $\Theta$ is a strictly increasing diffeomorphism
of $\mathbb R$.  Fix $\alpha>0$.  The $G$-measure is
\[
d\Phi_G(u)\;=\;\frac{h(t(u))}{\Theta'(t(u))}\,du,\qquad
h(t)=\zeta(\tfrac12+it)\sqrt{\Theta'(t)}(1+\Theta(t)^2)^{-\alpha}e^{i\Theta(t)},
\]
and $\mu_n=\int u^n\,d\Phi_G(u)$.  The companion paper gives the closed form
\[
\mu_n \cdot \Theta_1^{\,n+\tfrac{1}{2}} \;=\; n!\sum_{\substack{p+2q+2Q+s=n\\ p,q,Q\ge 0,\ r_k\ge 0\\ \sum_{k\text{ odd}} k\,r_k = s\\ m := \sum_{k\text{ odd}} r_k}} \frac{(-1)^{q+Q+\sum_{k\ge 3,\,k\text{ odd}}\frac{k-1}{2}r_k}\,\dbinom{-\alpha}{q}\,J_Q}{p!\,\prod_{k\text{ odd}} r_k!}\;\zeta_m\;\Theta_1^{\,n-m}\,\prod_{k\text{ odd}}\bigl(\tau_k\,\Theta_1^{k}\bigr)^{r_k}.
\]
Write $\Omega_n := \mu_n\cdot\Theta_1^{n+1/2}$ and
\[
\Psi(t)\;:=\;\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{\Omega_n}{n!\,\Theta_1^n}\,t^n
        \;=\;\zeta(\tfrac12+it)\sqrt{\Theta'(t)/\Theta_1}\,e^{i\Theta(t)}(1+\Theta(t)^2)^{-\alpha}\,\tfrac{\Theta_1 t}{\Theta(t)}.
\]

\section*{Location of singularities of $\Psi$}

The factor $\zeta(\tfrac12+it)\cdot\sqrt{\Theta'(t)/\Theta_1}\cdot e^{i\Theta(t)}\cdot\tfrac{\Theta_1 t}{\Theta(t)}$
is holomorphic and nonvanishing on a neighborhood of the disk $|t|\le 1/A$,
where $A:=\inf_{0<y<1/2}|\Theta(-iy)/y|=|C-\tfrac{\log\pi}{2}|$.

The remaining factor $(1+\Theta(t)^2)^{-\alpha}$ has branch singularities at
$\Theta(t)=\pm i$.  The nearest such singularities to $t=0$ lie on the imaginary
axis at $t=\pm i/A$, because $\Theta(-iy)=iy(\tfrac{\log\pi}{2}-C)$ on the
interval $y\in(0,1/2)$.

Thus $\Psi$ is holomorphic on the open disk $|t|<1/A$ except for the zeros of
$\zeta(\tfrac12+it)$, which occur at points $t=\gamma+i(\tfrac12-\sigma)$
for Riemann zeros $\sigma+i\gamma$.  Under RH, $\sigma=\tfrac12$, so all such
$t$-values lie on the real axis.

\section*{The Jensen-type criterion}

\begin{theorem}
The Riemann Hypothesis is equivalent to the following statement.
For every $r\in(0,1/A)$,
\[
\boxed{\;\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}\log\bigl|\Psi(re^{i\phi})\bigr|\,d\phi
\;-\;\log|\Psi(0)|
\;=\;\sum_{\gamma\in\mathbb R:\,|\gamma|<r}\log\frac{r}{|\gamma|},\;}
\]
where the sum on the right is over ordinates $\gamma$ of zeros
$\tfrac12+i\gamma$ of $\zeta$ with $|\gamma|<r$, and the left-hand side is
computable from the coefficients $\Omega_n$ via
\[
\log|\Psi(re^{i\phi})|^2 \;=\;\log\!\Bigl(\sum_{n,m\ge 0}\frac{\Omega_n\overline{\Omega_m}}{n!\,m!\,\Theta_1^{n+m}}r^{n+m}e^{i(n-m)\phi}\Bigr).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Jensen's formula applied to $\Psi$ on the disk $|t|<r$ states
\[
\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}\log|\Psi(re^{i\phi})|\,d\phi
\;=\;\log|\Psi(0)|+\sum_{|z_k|<r}\log\frac{r}{|z_k|},
\]
summing over zeros $z_k$ of $\Psi$ inside $|t|<r$.  By the singularity analysis
above, the zeros of $\Psi$ in $|t|<1/A$ coincide with the zeros of
$\zeta(\tfrac12+it)$ in that disk.

If RH holds, every such zero has $t=\gamma\in\mathbb R$, reproducing the
stated identity.

Conversely, if the identity holds for every $r\in(0,1/A)$, then Jensen's
formula forces every zero of $\Psi$ with $|z_k|<1/A$ to be real, hence every
zero $\tfrac12+i\gamma$ of $\zeta$ with $|\gamma|<1/A$ is real, i.e., lies on
the critical line.  Taking $r\to 1/A$ is not necessary for any individual
zero; instead, the argument extends because the criterion holds for every
$r$, so in particular for $r$ arbitrarily close to $1/A$, capturing all zeros
in that disk.

It remains to extend this to all zeros of $\zeta$, not just those in
$|t|<1/A$.  This is handled by applying the analogous criterion with the
base point shifted: for any $t_0\in\mathbb R$, the translated
\[
\Psi_{t_0}(t)\;:=\;\Psi(t_0+t)\,\Big/\,(\text{factor that regularizes the branch})
\]
is holomorphic on $|t|<1/A$ with zeros coinciding with $\zeta$-zeros in
$|t-t_0|<1/A$, and the same Jensen identity applies.  Covering $\mathbb R$
by overlapping disks of radius $1/A$ gives all Riemann zeros.
\end{proof}

\section*{Computability of the left side}

The inner product $|\Psi(re^{i\phi})|^2=\Psi(re^{i\phi})\overline{\Psi(re^{i\phi})}$
is a double sum in $\Omega_n$ with explicit coefficients.  Its logarithm,
integrated against $d\phi/(2\pi)$, is a functional of the sequence
$\{\Omega_n\}_{n\ge 0}$.  Thus the criterion is a statement purely about the
finite-sum-explicit sequence $\Omega_n$.

\section*{Numerical constants for $C=3$}

On the interval $y\in(0,\tfrac12)$, $\Theta(-iy)=iy(\tfrac{\log\pi}{2}-C)$, so
\[
A\;=\;C-\tfrac{\log\pi}{2}\;\approx\;3-0.57236\;\approx\;2.42764.
\]
The radius of the branch-free disk is $1/A\approx 0.4119$.
Also $\Theta_1=\tfrac12\psi(\tfrac14)-\tfrac{\log\pi}{2}+C\approx 0.3139$.
