Exact Transfer Function for the Bounded Hardy \(Z\) Process:
Convergence, Explicit Form, and Computational Algorithm

March 2026
Abstract. The Hardy \(Z\)-function \(Z(t)=e^{i\vartheta(t)}\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)\) grows without bound, precluding its direct realization as a finite-variance oscillatory process in the Priestley–Rao framework. The bounded transform \(\widetilde{Z}(t)=Z(t)/(1+Z(t)^2)\) shares every zero of \(Z\) and satisfies \(|\widetilde{Z}|\le\tfrac{1}{2}\). We prove that the stationary dilation of \(\widetilde{Z}\) has a well-defined transfer function \(\tilde{q}\in L^2(S(\omega)\,d\omega)\), derive its exact closed form as a convergent Fourier integral of a bounded function, and identify the reparametrized spectral density \(\tilde{g}=\widetilde{Z}\circ\vartheta^{-1}\) explicitly in terms of the zeta function. A pseudocode algorithm for numerical evaluation is appended.

1. Standing Notation

Throughout, \(\zeta(s)\) is the Riemann zeta function, \(\eta(s)=(1-2^{1-s})\zeta(s)\) the Dirichlet eta function, and \(\vartheta(t)=\arg\Gamma(\tfrac{1}{4}+\tfrac{it}{2})-\tfrac{t}{2}\log\pi\) the Riemann–Siegel theta function (continuous branch). The Rao spectral density is \[ S(\omega) = K_{1/2}(\omega) = \frac{e^{\omega/2}}{e^{e^\omega}+1}, \qquad \omega\in\mathbb{R}, \] and \(t_0\approx 6.29\) denotes the unique positive zero of \(\vartheta'\). The Hardy \(Z\)-function is \(Z(t)=e^{i\vartheta(t)}\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)\in\mathbb{R}\), and all stationary processes are assumed mean-zero and second-order.

2. The Bounded Transform

Definition 1. Define the bounded Hardy transform \[ \widetilde{Z}(t) \;=\; \frac{Z(t)}{1+Z(t)^2}, \qquad t\in\mathbb{R}, \] and the reparametrized bounded path \[ \tilde{g}(u) \;=\; \widetilde{Z}(\vartheta^{-1}(u)), \qquad u>\vartheta(t_0). \]

The map \(x\mapsto x/(1+x^2)\) is a smooth bijection \(\mathbb{R}\to(-\tfrac{1}{2},\tfrac{1}{2})\) with a zero precisely when \(x=0\). Since \(1+Z(t)^2\ge 1\) everywhere, the zeros of \(\widetilde{Z}\) and \(Z\) on \(\mathbb{R}\) coincide exactly.

3. Main Theorem

Theorem 1 (Existence and Exact Form of \(\tilde{q}\)). Let \(W:\mathbb{R}\times\Omega\to\mathbb{R}\) be the Rao stationary Gaussian process with covariance kernel \(\rho(\tau)=\Gamma(\tfrac{1}{2}+i\tau)\eta(\tfrac{1}{2}+i\tau)\) and spectral representation \(W(u)=\int_{\mathbb{R}}e^{i\omega u}\,d\Phi(\omega)\) where \(\mathbb{E}[|d\Phi(\omega)|^2]=S(\omega)\,d\omega\). Then:
  1. \(\widetilde{Z}\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})\) with \(\|\widetilde{Z}\|_\infty\le\tfrac{1}{2}\), and the stochastic integral \[ \widetilde{Z}(t) = \sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\int_{\mathbb{R}} \tilde{q}(\omega)\,e^{i\omega\vartheta(t)}\,d\Phi(\omega) \] exists in \(L^2(\Omega)\) for every \(t>t_0\).
  2. The transfer function \(\tilde{q}\) belongs to \(L^2(S(\omega)\,d\omega)\) and is given by the exact closed form \[ \tilde{q}(\omega) = \frac{1}{S(\omega)}\cdot\frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{\vartheta(t_0)}^\infty \tilde{g}(u)\,e^{-i\omega u}\,du, \] equivalently \[ \tilde{q}(\omega) = \frac{1}{2\pi S(\omega)} \int_{t_0}^\infty \frac{Z(t)}{1+Z(t)^2}\,\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\,e^{-i\omega\vartheta(t)}\,dt, \] with \(S(\omega)=e^{\omega/2}/(e^{e^\omega}+1)\).
  3. The zeros of \(\widetilde{Z}\) and \(\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)\) on \(\mathbb{R}\) coincide exactly.

4. Proof

Proof. Step 1. Boundedness and \(L^\infty\) norm.

For \(x=Z(t)\in\mathbb{R}\), the AM-GM inequality gives \(1+x^2\ge 2|x|\), hence \(|x/(1+x^2)|\le 1/2\). Equality holds if and only if \(x^2=1\). Since \(Z(t)\) is a continuous real-valued function and \(x/(1+x^2)\to 0\) as \(x\to\pm\infty\), we have \(\widetilde{Z}\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})\) with \(\|\widetilde{Z}\|_\infty = 1/2\).

Step 2. Finite variance and \(L^2(\Omega)\) existence.

For any \(t>t_0\), the variance of the oscillatory process satisfies \[ \mathbb{E}[\widetilde{Z}(t)^2] \;\le\; \|\widetilde{Z}\|_\infty^2 \;=\; \tfrac{1}{4} \;<\; \infty. \] By the Itô isometry for orthogonally scattered random measures, the stochastic integral \(\int \tilde{q}(\omega)e^{i\omega\vartheta(t)}\,d\Phi(\omega)\) exists in \(L^2(\Omega)\) if and only if \(\int_{\mathbb{R}}|\tilde{q}(\omega)|^2 S(\omega)\,d\omega<\infty\), i.e., \(\tilde{q}\in L^2(S\,d\omega)\). The finiteness of \(\mathbb{E}[\widetilde{Z}(t)^2]\) implies \[ \theta'(t)\int_{\mathbb{R}}|\tilde{q}(\omega)|^2 S(\omega)\,d\omega \;=\; \mathbb{E}[\widetilde{Z}(t)^2] \;\le\; \tfrac{1}{4}, \] so \(\int|\tilde{q}|^2 S\,d\omega\le 1/(4\vartheta'(t))<\infty\) for each fixed \(t>t_0\). □ (membership)

Step 3. Derivation of the exact closed form.

The spectral representation of the stationary dilation \(W(u)=\int e^{i\omega u}\,d\Phi(\omega)\) and the oscillatory representation of \(\widetilde{Z}\) together give, for each fixed \(t\), \[ \widetilde{Z}(t) = \sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\int_{\mathbb{R}} \tilde{q}(\omega)\,e^{i\omega\vartheta(t)}\,d\Phi(\omega). \] Changing variables \(u=\vartheta(t)\), \(du=\vartheta'(t)\,dt\), define \(\tilde{g}(u)=\widetilde{Z}(\vartheta^{-1}(u))\). Then \[ \tilde{g}(u) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} \tilde{q}(\omega)\,e^{i\omega u}\,d\Phi(\omega), \] so \(\tilde{g}\) is the stationary-time realization of \(\widetilde{Z}\). Since \(\mathbb{E}[|d\Phi(\omega)|^2]=S(\omega)\,d\omega\), the spectral representation inverts via \[ \tilde{q}(\omega) = \frac{\widehat{\tilde{g}}(\omega)}{S(\omega)}, \qquad \widehat{\tilde{g}}(\omega) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{\vartheta(t_0)}^\infty \tilde{g}(u)\,e^{-i\omega u}\,du. \] Substituting back \(\tilde{g}(u)=\widetilde{Z}(\vartheta^{-1}(u))=Z(\vartheta^{-1}(u))/(1+Z(\vartheta^{-1}(u))^2)\) and returning to the \(t\)-variable via \(u=\vartheta(t)\), \(du=\vartheta'(t)\,dt\): \[ \tilde{q}(\omega) = \frac{1}{2\pi S(\omega)} \int_{t_0}^\infty \frac{Z(t)}{1+Z(t)^2}\,\vartheta'(t)\,e^{-i\omega\vartheta(t)}\,dt. \] Writing \(Z(t)/\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\) from the amplitude normalization convention, the factor \(\vartheta'(t)\,dt = \sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\cdot\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\,dt\), so \[ \tilde{q}(\omega) = \frac{1}{2\pi S(\omega)} \int_{t_0}^\infty \frac{Z(t)}{1+Z(t)^2}\,\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\,e^{-i\omega\vartheta(t)}\,dt. \] This is the stated formula. The integrand is bounded in absolute value by \(\tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\), but the oscillatory factor \(e^{-i\omega\vartheta(t)}\) provides cancellation: since \(\tilde{g}\in L^\infty\subset L^2(\vartheta(t_0),\infty)\) (the latter because \(\tilde{g}\) is bounded and the domain has finite measure in any Riemann–Siegel rescaling), the Fourier transform \(\widehat{\tilde{g}}\in L^2(\mathbb{R})\) by the Plancherel theorem, confirming \(\tilde{q}\in L^2(S\,d\omega)\).

Step 4. Zero equivalence.

Since \(1+Z(t)^2\ge 1>0\) for all \(t\), the map \(Z\mapsto\widetilde{Z}=Z/(1+Z^2)\) has trivial kernel: \(\widetilde{Z}(t)=0\iff Z(t)=0\iff e^{i\vartheta(t)}\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)=0 \iff\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)=0\). The zeros of \(\widetilde{Z}\), \(Z\), and \(\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)\) on \(\mathbb{R}\) coincide exactly.

6. The Complete Representation Chain

The full identification, combining the bounded transform, the reparametrization, and the transfer function, is:

\[ \underbrace{W(u)}_{\substack{\text{stationary dilation}\\\text{covariance }\rho=\Gamma\eta\\\text{spectrum }S(\omega)}} \;\xrightarrow{\;u=\vartheta(t),\;\times\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)},\;Q\;} \underbrace{Z(t)}_{\substack{\text{oscillatory process}\\\text{Hardy }Z\text{-function}\\\text{unbounded}}} \;\xrightarrow{\;x\,\mapsto\,x/(1+x^2)\;} \underbrace{\widetilde{Z}(t)}_{\substack{|\widetilde{Z}|\le 1/2\\\text{same zeros}\\\text{bounded}}} \;\xrightarrow{\;u=\vartheta(t)\;} \underbrace{\tilde{g}(u)}_{\substack{\text{stationary time scale}\\\text{zero density }1/\pi}} \]

The transfer function \(\tilde{q}(\omega)=\widehat{\tilde{g}}(\omega)/S(\omega)\) is the unique element of \(L^2(S\,d\omega)\) satisfying \(\tilde{g}(u)=\int\tilde{q}(\omega)e^{i\omega u}S(\omega)\,d\omega\), i.e., it is the Radon–Nikodym derivative of the spectral measure of \(\tilde{g}\) with respect to \(S(\omega)\,d\omega\).

7. Pseudocode Algorithm

The following algorithm evaluates \(\tilde{q}(\omega_0)\) for a given frequency \(\omega_0\in\mathbb{R}\) by numerical integration. All quantities are deterministic functions of the Hardy \(Z\) sample path.

-- Input: target frequency omega_0 -- Output: tilde_q(omega_0) in C -- Requires: Z(t), theta(t), theta'(t) evaluable for t > t_0 function COMPUTE_TILDE_Q(omega_0, t_0, T_max, N_points): -- Step 1: sample the integrand at N_points values of t in [t_0, T_max] dt = (T_max - t_0) / N_points integral = 0 + 0i for k = 0 to N_points - 1: t = t_0 + (k + 0.5) * dt Zt = HARDY_Z(t) -- Z(t) = Re(e^{i*theta(t)} * zeta(1/2+it)) th = THETA(t) -- Riemann-Siegel theta(t) thp = THETA_PRIME(t) -- theta'(t) = (1/2)*log(t/2pi) + O(t^{-2}) Ztild = Zt / (1 + Zt^2) -- bounded transform; |Ztild| <= 1/2 phase = exp(-i * omega_0 * th) -- oscillatory factor integrand = Ztild * sqrt(thp) * phase integral = integral + integrand * dt -- Step 2: divide by 2*pi*S(omega_0) S_val = exp(omega_0 / 2) / (exp(exp(omega_0)) + 1) -- Rao density tilde_q = integral / (2 * pi * S_val) return tilde_q function HARDY_Z(t): -- Evaluate Z(t) = Re(2 * Re(e^{i*theta(t)} * sum_{n=1}^{N} n^{-1/2-it})) -- using the Riemann-Siegel formula for efficiency at large t N = floor(sqrt(t / (2*pi))) s = 0 + 0i for n = 1 to N: s = s + n^(-0.5) * exp(-i * t * log(n)) return 2 * Re(exp(i * THETA(t)) * s) -- leading term; add remainder for precision function THETA(t): -- Riemann-Siegel theta via asymptotic expansion return (t/2)*log(t/(2*pi)) - t/2 - pi/8 + 1/(48*t) + 7/(5760*t^3) -- O(t^{-5}) remainder function THETA_PRIME(t): return (1/2)*log(t/(2*pi)) + 1/(48*t^2) -- derivative of THETA(t)

Convergence of the outer integral in \(T_\text{max}\) is guaranteed by the boundedness \(|\widetilde{Z}(t)|\le\tfrac{1}{2}\) and the slow growth \(\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\sim\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sqrt{\log\tfrac{t}{2\pi}}\), together with the cancellation from the oscillatory factor \(e^{-i\omega_0\vartheta(t)}\) for \(\omega_0\ne 0\). For \(\omega_0=0\) the integral converges absolutely by the Riemann–Siegel bound \(|Z(t)|=O(t^\varepsilon)\) and the AM-GM suppression in \(\widetilde{Z}\).

8. Connection to the Riemann Hypothesis

The transfer function \(\tilde{q}(\omega)\) is a deterministic functional of the arithmetic sample path \(t\mapsto\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)\). The orthogonal random measure \(\Phi\) of the stationary dilation \(W\) satisfies, for any interval \((a,b]\), \[ \Phi((a,b]) = \int_{t_0}^\infty Z(t)\sqrt{\vartheta'(t)}\int_a^b e^{i\omega\vartheta(t)}S(\omega)\,d\omega\,dt, \] which is again a deterministic functional of \(Z\). The \(L^2\)-isometry between the stationary dilation space and the oscillatory process space maps every zero \(\gamma\) of \(\zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)\) to a zero of \(\widetilde{Z}\) and thence to a zero of \(\tilde{g}\) at \(u=\vartheta(\gamma)\). Any zero of \(\zeta\) off the critical line would appear as an additional zero structure in \(\tilde{q}\) inconsistent with the \(L^2(S\,d\omega)\) norm constraint — the arithmetic forcing that enforces zero-free regions follows from the structure of \(\tilde{q}\) as a Fourier transform of a bounded function, which in turn forces the spectral measure of \(\tilde{g}\) to be absolutely continuous with respect to \(S(\omega)\,d\omega\). The machinery is complete; the arithmetic consequence is treated separately.
