The oscillatory function is

$$
\phi_t(\xi) = \sqrt{\Psi(t)}\,e^{i\xi\psi(t)}.
$$

The gain is

$$
A_t(\xi) = \frac{\phi_t(\xi)}{e^{i\xi t}} = \sqrt{\Psi(t)}\,e^{i\xi(\psi(t)-t)}.
$$

The oscillatory process representation is

$$
\zeta(1/2+it) = \int_{-2}^{0} \phi_t(\xi)\,d\Phi(\xi) = \int_{-2}^{0} A_t(\xi)\,e^{i\xi t}\,d\Phi(\xi).
$$

The filter is the Fourier transform of the oscillatory function with respect to $$\xi$$:

$$
h_t(u) = \int_{-2}^{0} \phi_t(\xi)\,e^{i\xi u}\,d\xi = \sqrt{\Psi(t)}\int_{-2}^{0} e^{i\xi(\psi(t)+u)}\,d\xi = 2\sqrt{\Psi(t)}\,e^{-i(\psi(t)+u)}\,\mathrm{sinc}(\psi(t)+u).
$$

The time-varying filter representation is

$$
\zeta(1/2+it) = \int h_t(u)\,S(t-u)\,du,
$$

where $$S(t) = \int_{-2}^{0} e^{i\xi t}\,d\Phi(\xi)$$ is the stationary process with spectrum $$d\mu$$ on $$[-2,0]$$.

The evolutionary power spectrum (Priestley, Definition 4.1) is

$$
dF_t(\xi) = |A_t(\xi)|^2\,d\mu(\xi) = \Psi(t)\,d\mu(\xi).
$$

The autocovariance is

$$
R(s,t) = \int_{-2}^{0} \phi_s(\xi)\,\overline{\phi_t(\xi)}\,d\mu(\xi) = \sqrt{\Psi(s)\Psi(t)}\int_{-2}^{0} e^{i\xi(\psi(s)-\psi(t))}\,d\mu(\xi).
$$

The Hilbert transform of the filter with respect to $$u$$:

$$
\mathcal{H}[h_t](u) = \int_{-2}^{0} \phi_t(\xi)\left[-i\,\mathrm{sgn}(\xi)\right]e^{i\xi u}\,d\xi.
$$

For $$\xi \in (-2,0)$$, $$\mathrm{sgn}(\xi)=-1$$, so

$$
\mathcal{H}[h_t](u) = i\int_{-2}^{0} \phi_t(\xi)\,e^{i\xi u}\,d\xi = i\,h_t(u).
$$

Positive-frequency projection of the filter:

$$
h_t^+(u) = \tfrac{1}{2}(h_t(u) + i\cdot i\,h_t(u)) = \tfrac{1}{2}(h_t(u) - h_t(u)) = 0.
$$

The filter has no positive-frequency content. The one-sided spectrum and the causality of the filter are the same statement.
