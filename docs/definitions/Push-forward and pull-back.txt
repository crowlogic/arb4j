Remark (Terminology). In the standard RKHS convention,
ζ(1/2+it) = √ψ′(t) · X(ψ(t)) is called the pullback of X
through ψ. This labels the complicated non-stationary object
as a pullback of the simple stationary one — as though
introducing all the amplitude modulation and time-warping
were a simplification. It is equally correct to take ψ⁻¹ as
the reference map, under which X = (ζ ∘ ψ⁻¹)/√(ψ′ ∘ ψ⁻¹)
is the pullback of ζ through ψ⁻¹, and ζ is the pushforward
of X through ψ⁻¹. Pullback along ψ⁻¹ goes from complicated
to simple; pushforward along ψ⁻¹ goes from simple to
complicated. Therefore ψ⁻¹ is adopted as the reference map:
X is the pullback of ζ through ψ⁻¹, and ζ is the pushforward
of X through ψ⁻¹.
