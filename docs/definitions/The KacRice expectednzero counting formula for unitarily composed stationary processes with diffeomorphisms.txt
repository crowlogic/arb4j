Theorem. Let X be a zero-mean stationary Gaussian process on ℝ with
covariance R(τ) = E[X(u)X(u+τ)], where R ∈ C². Let θ: ℝ → ℝ be a
C² diffeomorphism with θ′ > 0. Define

  Y(t) = √θ′(t) · X(θ(t)).

Then the expected number of zeros of Y on [0,T] is

  E[N_Y(0,T)] = ν₀ · (θ(T) − θ(0)),

where ν₀ = (1/π)√(−R″(0)/R(0)).

Proof.

The covariance of Y is

  C(t,s) = √(θ′(t)θ′(s)) · R(θ(t) − θ(s)).

Evaluating on the diagonal gives

  C(t,t) = θ′(t) · R(0).

By the product rule and the chain rule, the derivative of Y is

  Y′(t) = θ″(t)/(2θ′(t)) · Y(t) + θ′(t)√θ′(t) · X′(θ(t)).

Since R is even, R′(0) = 0, hence E[X(u)X′(u)] = R′(0) = 0 for all u.
Therefore Y(t) and X′(θ(t)) are uncorrelated. The expression above
factorizes Y′(t) into θ″(t)/(2θ′(t)) · Y(t), which vanishes when
Y(t) = 0, and θ′(t)√θ′(t) · X′(θ(t)), which is uncorrelated with
Y(t). The conditional variance is therefore

  Var[Y′(t) | Y(t) = 0] = θ′(t)² · θ′(t) · Var[X′(θ(t))]
                         = θ′(t)² · θ′(t) · (−R″(0)).

Substituting into the Kac–Rice formula for the zero-crossing intensity,

  ν(t) = (1/π) · √(Var[Y′(t) | Y(t)=0] / C(t,t))
       = (1/π) · √(θ′(t)² · θ′(t) · (−R″(0)) / (θ′(t) · R(0)))
       = (θ′(t)/π) · √(−R″(0)/R(0))
       = θ′(t) · ν₀.

Integrating over [0,T],

  E[N_Y(0,T)] = ∫₀ᵀ ν(t) dt = ν₀ ∫₀ᵀ θ′(t) dt = ν₀ · (θ(T) − θ(0)).  ∎
